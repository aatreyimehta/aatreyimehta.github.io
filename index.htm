Project Outline:

Crohn's disease (CD) is becoming increasingly common, necessitating the development of more accurate and less intrusive diagnostic methods. Faecal Calprotectin (FC) concentrations are a reliable biomarker of luminal inflammatory processes and can be used instead of invasive and unpleasant ileocolonoscopies. FC levels have been linked to the progression of CD in studies, and several machine learning algorithms have been applied to predict disease progression. The goal of this research is to compare the efficacy of established machine learning algorithms like logistic regression, support vector machine, random forests, and artificial neural networks, for predicting CD progression using a variety of indicators, including FC levels. Based on certain records of FC measurement of patients with CD, it was revealed that all four techniques performed well, demonstrating their utility in forecasting illness progression. Although, it was observed that logistic regression slightly surpassed the others. Due to missing data for a number of patients in the dataset, fewer variables were chosen for inclusion in the model. Due to the limited sample size, SVM, Random Forest, and the ANN did not show superior performance in the investigation when compared to logistic regression. In this paper, I intend to analyse more variables and validate my findings using another big and independent dataset.
